Strand 4: Natural Resources · Units 1–3

Capture Fishery: Cultivation & Regulations

Grade 11 Agriculture

Learning Objectives

  • Examine capture fishery cultivation in different natural environments
  • Explain the fishery marketing process and price determination in PNG
  • Interpret PNG fisheries policies, rules, and regulations

Capture Fishery Environments in PNG

  • Sea ranching — juvenile fish raised in hatcheries are released into coastal waters to grow naturally and be harvested later.
  • Coastal lagoon farming — shallow enclosed coastal lagoons raise milkfish, mullet, and shrimp.
  • Stocking of inland lagoons — freshwater lagoons, lakes, and dams are stocked with fish for subsistence and commercial harvest.
  • Flood plain fisheries — PNG river flood plains (Fly River, Sepik) support rich seasonal fish populations managed for sustainable harvest.

Fish Markets and Price Determination

Fish distribution in PNG involves three market levels:

  • Primary markets — near fishing areas; fishers sell directly.
  • Secondary markets — intermediaries transport fish to port markets and wholesalers.
  • City/terminal markets — retailers sell directly to consumers (e.g. Koki Fish Market, Port Moresby).

Price factors: transport cost, production cost, supply and demand, competition, processing technique, and species variety.

PNG Fisheries Regulations

The Fisheries Management Act 1998 governs all PNG fisheries. The NFA manages, controls, and regulates all fishery resources and issues licences.

PNG\'s EEZ under the UN Convention on the Law of the Sea extends 200 nautical miles and gives PNG sovereign rights over vast marine resources.

Example regulations for Barramundi: prohibited net mesh sizes during peak spawning (Oct–Nov); total allowable catch of 260 metric tonnes per year.

Key Terms

Capture fisheryThe harvesting of wild fish and aquatic organisms from natural environments — seas, rivers, and lakes.
Sea ranchingReleasing hatchery-raised juvenile fish into the sea to grow wild and be harvested later — a form of stock enhancement.

Summary

  • Capture fishery involves harvesting wild fish from seas, rivers, lagoons, and flood plains
  • PNG capture environments include coastal seas, lagoons, inland rivers, and mangrove areas
