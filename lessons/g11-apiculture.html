🐝 Strand 2: Animals · Unit 1

Apiculture: Beekeeping in PNG

📚 Grade 11 🌿 Agriculture

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the terminology of apiculture and identify types of insects used for it
  • Examine the purposes, cultivation, and management systems of apiculture
  • Discuss the methods and processes used in apiculture production in PNG

What is Apiculture?

Apiculture is the practice of keeping and managing honeybee colonies in hives. It provides bees with a safe environment and is important because bees pollinate many of our food sources. Beekeepers keep bees for:

  • Honey production — high nutritional and commercial value.
  • Beeswax production — used in cosmetics, candles, and traditional medicines.
  • Crop pollination — hives placed in orchards and gardens improve yields.
  • Sale of bees — producing and selling colonies to other beekeepers.

Cultivation and Management Practices

  • Site selection — non-humid areas, away from cold winds, pollution, and industrial areas.
  • Hive identification — each hive is given a unique numerical code for tracking.
  • Regular health checks — inspect colonies regularly; reduce during winter or unfavourable weather.
  • Comb renewal — replace honeycombs every two years for hygiene and productivity.
  • Queen replacement — replace queens every 1–2 years to maintain vigour.
  • Quarantine measures — quarantine any new introductions to prevent disease spread.

Benefits of Apiculture for PNG Communities

  • Income generation — honey and beeswax are high-value products for local sale or export.
  • Low capital requirement — can be started on a small scale with minimal investment.
  • Environmental benefit — bees pollinate native plants and food crops.
  • Nutritional value — honey is nutritious and used in traditional healing.
  • Sustainable livelihood — does not require land clearing; coexists with forest conservation.

Key Terms

ApicultureThe science and practice of keeping and managing honeybee colonies in hives for honey, beeswax, or pollination.
ApiaryA place where beehives are kept — also called a bee yard. Location is critical for colony health and productivity.
Queen beeThe single fertile female bee in a colony, responsible for all egg-laying. Replace every 1–2 years for productivity.
HoneycombThe wax structure built by bees inside the hive, made up of hexagonal cells used for honey, pollen, and larvae.
PollinationThe transfer of pollen enabling plant reproduction — a critical ecological service provided by bees.

Summary

  • Apiculture (beekeeping) is the practice of keeping and managing honeybee colonies in hives
  • Purposes include honey, beeswax, crop pollination, and the sale of bee colonies
  • Good site selection, regular inspections, comb renewal, and queen replacement are key practices
  • Bees provide the critical ecological service of pollination, supporting crop production and biodiversity
  • Apiculture offers low-capital, sustainable income opportunities for PNG communities
  • Quarantine must be applied to any new introductions to the apiary to prevent disease
