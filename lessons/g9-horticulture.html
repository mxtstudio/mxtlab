🍅 Strand 1: Crops · Unit 5

Horticulture — Fruits, Vegetables & Spices

Grade 9 Papua New Guinea

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Explain what horticulture is and identify the types of horticultural plants
  • Describe how fruit trees, vegetables, and spices are cultivated and processed
  • Explain how horticultural plant products are preserved and marketed

What is Horticulture?

Horticulture is a branch of plant agriculture that deals with garden crops — fruits, vegetables, and ornamental plants. The word comes from the Latin hortus (garden) and cultura (cultivation).

Horticultural plants need their seeds planted in a seedbed and are looked after for some weeks before being transplanted to the field. Irrigation and nursery management are very important — crops cannot be successfully produced where water is insufficient.

The Three Main Types of Horticultural Plants

  • Fruit trees — trees which bear fruits consumed by humans and animals. Examples: mango, pawpaw, citrus, avocado, pineapple
  • Vegetables — the edible parts of plants including flower, fruit, stem, or leaf. Examples: tomato, cabbage, beans, pumpkin, spinach
  • Spices — seeds, fruits, roots, bark, or other plant substances used mainly for flavouring, colouring, or preserving food. Examples: ginger, chilli, turmeric, pepper, vanilla

Importance and Benefits of Horticulture

  • Provides nutritious food — fruits and vegetables supply vitamins, minerals, and fibre
  • Provides income — horticultural products are sold at local markets and can be exported
  • Can be grown on small plots, making it suitable for subsistence and smallholder farmers
  • Improves food security at family and community level
  • Provides raw materials for food processing industries
  • Ornamental horticulture improves the environment and supports tourism

Fruit Tree Cultivation

Fruit trees are cultivated in different ways depending on the environment:

  • Seedling propagation — growing trees from seeds. Slower but inexpensive
  • Grafting — joining a shoot from a productive tree onto a strong rootstock. Produces fruit faster and maintains desired characteristics
  • Budding — inserting a single bud from one tree into the bark of another. Used for citrus, mango, and avocado
  • Cuttings — using stem sections to grow new plants. Used for cassava, sugarcane, and some fruits

Good fruit tree cultivation also requires: correct spacing, regular pruning, weed control, fertilising, pest and disease management, and irrigation during dry periods.

Vegetable Cultivation

Vegetables need fertile, well-drained soil with consistent moisture. Key steps in vegetable cultivation:

  1. Land preparation — clearing, tilling, and improving the soil
  2. Seedbed preparation — creating a fine, level bed for planting
  3. Planting — direct seeding or transplanting seedlings raised in a nursery
  4. Irrigation — especially during dry periods
  5. Fertilising — organic compost or appropriate fertilisers
  6. Pest and disease management — monitoring and treating as needed
  7. Harvesting — at the right stage of maturity for best quality

Processing, Preserving and Marketing of Horticultural Products

After harvest, horticultural products must be handled carefully to maintain quality:

  • Processing — cleaning, sorting, grading, and sometimes cooking or drying products
  • Preservation — extending the shelf life through drying, pickling, freezing, canning, or smoking
  • Packaging — using appropriate containers to protect produce during transport
  • Marketing — selling at markets, roadside stalls, to wholesalers, or through cooperatives
🇵🇬 PNG Example: Spice crops like vanilla and ginger are important horticultural exports from Papua New Guinea. They require careful post-harvest handling to maintain quality for export markets.

📖 Key Terms

HorticultureA branch of agriculture dealing with garden crops including fruits, vegetables, and ornamental plants
GraftingA propagation technique where a shoot from one plant is joined onto the rootstock of another to grow as a single plant
NurseryA place where young plants are raised from seeds before transplanting to the field
ProcessingPreparing a harvested product for sale or further use — including cleaning, sorting, grading, and packaging
PreservationExtending the shelf life of a food product through methods such as drying, freezing, pickling, or canning

📌 Summary

  • Horticulture covers fruits, vegetables, spices, and ornamental plants
  • Horticultural plants provide nutrition, income, and food security
  • Fruit trees can be propagated by seeds, grafting, budding, or cuttings
  • Vegetable cultivation requires fertile soil, irrigation, fertilising, and pest management
  • Post-harvest handling — processing, preservation, packaging — is essential to maintain quality and earn good prices
