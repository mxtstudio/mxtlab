Crop Management Systems & Practices

📖 3 lessons 🇵🇬 Papua New Guinea Curriculum
Crop Management Practices Crop Cultivation Principles Organic Farming Principles

Learning Objectives

  • Describe crop management systems and practices used in different contexts
  • Explain crop cultivation and management principles including pest control, irrigation, pruning, and fertilisation
  • Describe the four guiding principles of organic farming

Importance of Crop Management

Adoption of best crop management practices improves crop productivity and contributes to greater yields with improved quality. Crop management is the set of agricultural practices performed to improve the growth, development, and yield of crops. It begins with seedbed preparation and sowing of seeds, continues through crop maintenance, and ends with harvest, storage, and marketing.

Key Crop Management Practices

  • Seedbed preparation — soil must be prepared to the right texture and condition for good seed germination.
  • Planting — seeds should be sown at the correct depth (usually 1.5 to 2.0 inches) to ensure proper moisture for germination.
  • Fertilisation — soils should be tested for available nutrients before applying fertilisers.
  • Pest management — pesticides and cultural practices control pests that threaten crop yield and quality.
  • Irrigation — the artificial application of water, critical in dry regions to maintain moisture for crop production.
  • Pruning — removing parts of the plant not required for growth; also serves as preventive maintenance against insects and disease.
  • Drainage — proper removal of excess water prevents waterlogging and root disease.
  • Harvesting — yield and quality depend on the harvest management strategy and timing.
  • Post-harvest storage — proper storage conditions maintain forage and grain quality after harvest.

Additional Best Practices

  • Increase crop diversity across the farm
  • Follow crop rotation to preserve soil health
  • Add nutrients based on soil testing results and crop needs
  • Keep accurate records of expenses and profits

The Four Principles of Organic Farming

Organic farming provides a vision for agriculture that inspires environmentally friendly cultivation. The four guiding principles are:

  • Health — organic agriculture sustains and enhances the health of soils, plants, animals, humans, and the planet as one indivisible whole.
  • Fairness — organic agriculture builds relationships that ensure fairness with regard to the common environment and opportunities for all.
  • Ecology — organic agriculture is based on living ecological systems and cycles. It includes recycling and caring for the ecosystem — protecting air, water, biodiversity, climate, and land.
  • Care — organic agriculture should be managed in a precautionary and responsible manner to protect the health and wellbeing of current and future generations.

Key Terms

Seedbed preparationThe first step in crop management — preparing soil to the right texture and condition for seed germination.
IrrigationThe artificial application of water to the soil through systems of tubes, pumps, and sprays to supplement rainfall.
PruningRemoval or reduction of parts of plant crops not required for growth or production; also helps prevent insect and disease damage.
Pest managementReducing or eliminating unwanted organisms that threaten crop health and yield.
Crop rotationAlternating different crops on the same land each season to preserve soil nutrients, reduce disease, and prevent erosion.
Organic farmingFarming based on the four principles of health, fairness, ecology, and care, avoiding synthetic chemicals.

📝 Summary — What You Should Know

  • Crop management begins with seedbed preparation and ends with harvest, storage, and marketing
  • Key practices include fertilisation (based on soil testing), pest management, irrigation, pruning, and drainage
  • Crop rotation alternates different crops each season to preserve soil nutrients and reduce disease risk
  • Good record-keeping of expenses and profits is essential for a profitable farm business
  • The four principles of organic farming are: Health, Fairness, Ecology, and Care
  • Organic farming avoids synthetic chemicals and aims to sustain soil, plant, animal, and human health
