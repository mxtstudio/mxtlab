Strand 4: Natural Resources · Units 4–6

Forest Products, Regulations & Technology

Grade 11 Agriculture

Learning Objectives

  • Evaluate approaches and practices for producing and preserving forest products
  • Explain how the forestry industry in PNG is regulated
  • Identify technologies used in growing timber species in PNG

Forest Products and Preservation

Forests provide a wide range of products:

  • Wood products — timber, sandalwood, plywood.
  • Non-wood forest products (NWFP) — bamboo, honey, medicinal plants, kendu leaves.
  • Industrial products — rubber, cashews, spices, biodiesel plants.

Steps to preserve forest resources: regulated and planned cutting; fire control; reforestation and afforestation; protecting key areas (national parks); and proper utilisation to minimise waste.

PNG Forestry Regulations

  • Forestry Act 1991 — makes provision for the management, development, and protection of PNG\'s forest resources.
  • PNG Forest Authority (PNGFA) — monitors and controls the wood industry and manages PNG\'s forests.

Forest policy addresses: rent capture from timber harvesting, market-based pricing, domestic processing promotion, and sustainable development investment.

Technology in Timber Growing

  • Drones — aerial surveys, mapping, detecting damaged trees.
  • LiDAR — shoots laser beams from the air creating precise 3D maps of forests.
  • GIS — Geographic Information Systems integrating data to produce detailed forest maps.
  • Advanced tree-breeding — selecting the hardiest-growing trees for seedling production.
  • Controlled nurseries — greenhouses with precise water, light, and space management for healthier seedlings.
  • Digital growth monitoring — programs that track forest growth and predict future yields.

Key Terms

Non-wood forest products (NWFP)Products from forests other than timber — including fruits, honey, bamboo, and medicinal plants.
PNGFAPapua New Guinea Forest Authority — the government body responsible for monitoring and controlling the forest industry.
LiDARLight Detection and Ranging — laser technology that creates detailed digital maps of forest structure and terrain from the air.
ReforestationPlanting trees in areas where forests have been removed or depleted.

Summary

  • Forests provide timber, non-wood products (honey, bamboo, medicinal plants), and industrial products (rubber, spices)
  • Forest preservation includes regulated logging, fire control, reforestation, and protection of key areas
