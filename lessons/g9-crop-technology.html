⚙️ Strand 1: Crops · Unit 4

Crop Farming Technology

Grade 9 Papua New Guinea

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Identify the advantages and disadvantages of technology used in crop cultivation
  • Describe different types of technologies used in crop management
  • Analyse the benefits of technology in crop management

Technology in Crop Cultivation

Technology has changed farming significantly. Modern machines, irrigation systems, and digital tools allow farmers to produce more food on the same land. However, technology also comes with challenges.

Advantages of Technology in Crop Cultivation

  • Modern machinery reduces the physical effort required by farmers and saves time
  • Irrigation technology supplies water to crops efficiently, even in dry conditions
  • Machines help with sowing seeds accurately, at the right spacing and depth
  • Technology is used in transportation of produce to markets
  • Application of synthetic fertilisers can boost soil nutrients quickly
  • Chemical pest control can prevent large crop losses from insects and diseases
  • Technology increases the price and demand of products through better marketing
  • Online trading and e-commerce helps farmers sell directly to buyers
  • Precision farming reduces the use of water and fertiliser, keeping prices down
  • Technology reduces the run-off of chemicals into water sources

Disadvantages of Technology in Crop Cultivation

  • Excessive use of chemicals (through machines) reduces the long-term fertility of the land
  • Farmers without training cannot handle machines properly — leading to poor results or accidents
  • The cost of purchasing and maintaining machinery is very high
  • Overuse of machines can lead to environmental damage — soil compaction, erosion
  • Technology has many side effects if not used correctly
  • Most farmers in developing countries may lack literacy to use modern technologies
💡 Key Insight: The best approach is to combine traditional knowledge with appropriate modern technology. Technology should match the scale and resources of the farmer.

Precision Farming and Crop Management Technology

Precision farming is a systems approach to managing crops and land selectively according to their specific needs. It integrates information technology with farming to help managers make better decisions about each part of their fields.

Types of crop management technologies include:

  • GPS and mapping systems — help farmers track and manage different sections of their farm
  • Soil sensors — measure moisture, pH, and nutrient levels in real time
  • Drones — used to survey large crop areas, detect disease, and apply pesticides efficiently
  • Drip irrigation systems — deliver water directly to plant roots, reducing waste
  • Weather forecasting tools — help farmers plan planting and harvesting around expected conditions

📖 Key Terms

Precision farmingA systems approach that uses technology to manage crops and land selectively according to their specific needs
Irrigation technologyAny system used to supply water artificially to crops — e.g. drip irrigation, sprinkler systems
Soil compactionWhen heavy machinery presses soil particles together, reducing pore space and making it harder for roots to grow
E-commerceBuying and selling goods and services over the internet

📌 Summary

  • Technology in agriculture includes machinery, irrigation systems, GPS, sensors, and drones
  • Key advantages: saves labour and time, boosts yield, improves marketing and distribution
  • Key disadvantages: high cost, potential environmental damage, requires training and literacy
  • Precision farming uses information technology to manage specific parts of a farm more effectively
  • The best farming combines appropriate technology with traditional knowledge and sustainable practices
