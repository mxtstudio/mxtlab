📣 Strand 5: Agribusiness · Unit 2

Agribusiness Communication & Marketing

Grade 10 Papua New Guinea

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Describe the types of markets related to agribusiness
  • Explain key marketing principles for agricultural products
  • Describe the components of the sales process in agribusiness

Types of Agricultural Markets

A market is any place or mechanism where buyers and sellers meet to exchange goods and services. Agricultural markets can be:

  • Local markets — village markets, town markets, roadside stalls. Most smallholder farmers in PNG sell here. Prices are local and vary daily.
  • Wholesale markets — where traders buy large quantities from farmers to sell on to retailers or processors. Examples: the Lae and Port Moresby produce markets.
  • Supermarkets / retail — formal retail where products must meet quality and packaging standards
  • Export markets — selling agricultural products to buyers in other countries. Requires quality certification, correct packaging, and compliance with import regulations
  • Online markets — selling via social media, websites, or digital platforms. Growing in PNG particularly for handicrafts and specialty food products

The 4 Ps of Marketing

Marketing is about getting the right product to the right customer at the right price through the right channels. The classic framework is the 4 Ps:

  • Product — what are you selling? What makes it valuable? Quality, size, freshness, variety all matter.
  • Price — how much will you charge? Price must cover costs, be competitive, and reflect value
  • Place — where and how will you sell? Local market, cooperative, wholesale buyer, or export?
  • Promotion — how will people know about your product? Word of mouth, signs, social media, or radio advertising?

Sales Strategies for Agribusiness

  • Timing of sales — avoid selling immediately after harvest when all farmers are selling and prices are lowest. Store where possible and sell when prices recover
  • Value-adding — processing raw products before selling increases their value. Turning fresh cocoa into fermented, dried beans increases the price; packaging fresh vegetables attractively increases perceived value
  • Building relationships with buyers — reliable, consistent supply builds trust with buyers and can secure better ongoing prices
  • Collective selling — selling as a group through a cooperative gives farmers more bargaining power
  • Quality standards — maintaining quality (grading, proper handling, clean presentation) commands better prices

The Sales Process

The sales process describes the steps from identifying a potential customer to completing a sale:

  1. Prospecting — identifying potential buyers (market traders, processors, export buyers)
  2. Preparation — preparing the product (grading, packaging, calculating minimum acceptable price)
  3. Approach — making initial contact with potential buyers and building rapport
  4. Presentation — showing and describing the product, emphasising its quality and value
  5. Handling objections — responding to concerns about price, quality, or quantity
  6. Closing the sale — agreeing on a price and quantity and completing the transaction
  7. Follow-up — checking that the buyer is satisfied and maintaining the relationship for future sales

📖 Key Terms

MarketingThe activities involved in promoting and selling a product — from identifying customers to completing the sale
Value-addingProcessing or improving a raw agricultural product to increase its market value before selling
The 4 PsA marketing framework: Product, Price, Place, and Promotion
Wholesale marketA market where large quantities are bought and sold between traders rather than directly to consumers
ProspectingThe first step of the sales process — identifying and approaching potential buyers

📌 Summary

  • Agricultural markets range from local village markets to export markets — each has different requirements
  • The 4 Ps of marketing are Product, Price, Place, and Promotion
  • Timing sales, value-adding, building buyer relationships, and maintaining quality all improve sales outcomes
  • Collective selling through cooperatives gives individual farmers more market power
  • The sales process: prospecting → preparation → approach → presentation → handling objections → closing → follow-up
