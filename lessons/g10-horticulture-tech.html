Technology in Horticulture Production

📖 3 lessons 🇵🇬 Papua New Guinea Curriculum
Processing & Preservation Technology Packaging & Transportation Marketing Technology & Regulation

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and explain how technology is used in the processing and preservation of horticulture products
  • Describe packaging and transportation technology used for horticulture products
  • Explain how technology is used in the marketing and regulation of horticulture products

Processing and Preservation Technology

Horticultural products are highly perishable. Technology is used to slow deterioration and extend shelf life.

Processing Technologies

  • Washing and cleaning equipment — mechanical washers remove soil and pesticide residues from fruit and vegetables before packaging.
  • Grading and sorting machines — optical sensors and weight-based machines sort produce by size, colour, and quality.
  • Drying and dehydration — solar dryers and mechanical dryers remove moisture from fruits, vegetables, and spices to extend shelf life.
  • Juicing and processing — equipment to extract juice, make sauces, jams, and preserves from surplus or lower-grade fruit.

Preservation Technologies

  • Refrigeration and cold storage — the most important preservation technology. Cold storage slows biological processes that cause spoilage.
  • Modified atmosphere packaging (MAP) — adjusts oxygen, carbon dioxide, and nitrogen levels inside packaging to slow ripening and decay.
  • Vacuum packaging — removes air from packaging to prevent oxidation and spoilage.
  • Wax coating — edible wax applied to fruits to reduce moisture loss and extend shelf life.

Packaging and Transportation Technology

  • Corrugated cardboard boxes — the standard for packing and stacking fruit and vegetables for export. Ventilated to allow airflow.
  • Clamshell packs and flow-wrapping — used for small fruits, berries, and vegetables in retail settings.
  • Refrigerated transport (cool trucks and reefer containers) — maintain cold temperatures during transport. Essential for export of fresh produce from PNG.
  • Tracking systems — GPS and barcode systems track produce through the supply chain from farm to retailer.

Marketing Technology and Regulation

Marketing Technology

  • E-commerce platforms — online marketplaces allow farmers to list and sell products directly to buyers, reducing dependence on middlemen.
  • Social media marketing — Facebook and similar platforms are widely used in PNG to advertise fresh produce and connect with buyers.
  • Mobile money — payment apps allow farmers in remote areas to receive payment digitally.

Food Regulation

Government regulations set standards for the quality, safety, and labelling of horticulture products. In Papua New Guinea, the National Agriculture and Quarantine Inspection Authority (NAQIA) regulates the import and export of agricultural products, enforcing biosecurity rules and quality standards. Compliance with food safety regulations is essential for accessing export markets.

Key Terms

Post-harvest processingAll steps taken after a crop is harvested to prepare it for sale — including cleaning, grading, packaging, and storage.
Cold chainA temperature-controlled supply chain that keeps perishable horticultural products cold from harvest through to the consumer.
GradingSorting harvested produce into categories based on size, weight, colour, and quality — essential for commercial marketing.
Modified atmosphere packagingPackaging technology that alters the gas composition inside a package to extend the shelf life of fresh produce.
E-commerceBuying and selling goods and services online. Increasingly used by PNG farmers to reach direct buyers and markets.
Food regulationGovernment rules that set standards for the quality, safety, labelling, and export of food and agricultural products.

📝 Summary — What You Should Know

  • Post-harvest processing includes washing, grading, drying, and packaging to prepare produce for sale
  • Cold storage is the most important preservation technology, slowing spoilage by keeping produce cold
  • Modified atmosphere packaging (MAP) adjusts gas composition inside packaging to slow ripening and decay
  • Refrigerated transport (cool trucks and reefer containers) maintains cold temperatures during delivery
  • E-commerce and social media allow PNG farmers to sell directly to buyers, reducing reliance on middlemen
  • NAQIA regulates PNG agricultural imports and exports, enforcing biosecurity and quality standards
