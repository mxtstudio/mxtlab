Types of Agricultural Plants

📖 3 lessons 🇵🇬 Papua New Guinea Curriculum
Plantation Crops Ornamental Plants Bedding Plants & Classification

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and describe the major types of plantation crops and their uses
  • Classify ornamental plants and explain their characteristics and roles in horticulture
  • Describe the four categories of bedding plants and their cultivation contexts

Plantation Crops

Plantation crops constitute a large group of commercial crops. The major plantation crops include coconut, oil palm, cashew, tea, coffee, and rubber; the minor plantation crops include cocoa. They are classified as follows:

  • Beverage crops — crops processed into beverages: tea, coffee, cocoa
  • Oil yielding crops — crops grown to extract oil: coconut, oil palm
  • Nuts — dry single-seeded fruits with high oil content: cashew
  • Industrial crops — crops used in industrial production: rubber
  • Masticatory crops — crops used for chewing: arecanut, betel vine

In Papua New Guinea, oil palm, coconut, cocoa, and coffee are all significant plantation crops that contribute substantially to agricultural export revenue.

Ornamental Plants

Ornamental plants are grown for decorative purposes. Their cultivation is called floriculture. They are divided into two main categories:

A. Herbaceous Ornamental Plants (soft-stemmed)

  • Annuals — Winter season: Dahlia, Poppy, Dianthus, Marigold. Summer: Balsam, Cockscomb. All-season: Sunflower, Zinnia.
  • Biennials — Hollyhock, Sweet William.
  • Perennials — for flowers: Phlox, Gladiolus.
  • Aquatic ornamentals — Lotus, water lily, red water lily.
  • Orchids — Vanda, Dendrobium, Vanilla.

B. Woody Ornamental Plants (hard-stemmed)

  • Flowering shrubs: Rose, Jasmine, China rose
  • Ornamental palms: Bottle palm, Fishtail palm, Oil palm, Chinese palm
  • Creepers and climbers: Bougainvillea

Bedding Plants

Bedding plants are categorised into four main groups:

  • Hardy annuals — Grown directly early in the season. Examples: poppy, sunflower, dianthus.
  • Tender annual (perennial) plants — Planted as young plants after frost. Examples: begonia, petunia, chrysanthemum, fuchsia.
  • Hardy biennial plants — Discarded after flowering. Examples: daisy, foxglove, cornflower, pansies.
  • Corms or tubers — Planted each year and lifted after the plant naturally dies. Examples: narcissus, hyacinth, gladiolus.

Key Terms

Plantation cropA large-scale commercial crop grown on a plantation. Major PNG plantation crops include coconut, oil palm, tea, coffee, and cocoa.
Beverage cropCrops whose products are processed into beverages other than water — e.g., tea, coffee, and cocoa.
Ornamental plantA plant grown for decorative purposes in landscaping, gardens, or indoor settings rather than for food or industrial use.
FloricultureThe cultivation of ornamental plants. Forms a major branch of horticulture.
Herbaceous ornamental plantAn ornamental plant with a soft, non-woody stem. Examples include annuals, biennials, perennials, and aquatic ornamentals.
Bedding plantPlants used to create colourful displays in garden beds — categorised as hardy annuals, tender annuals, hardy biennials, and corms or tubers.

📝 Summary — What You Should Know

  • Major plantation crops in PNG include coconut, oil palm, cocoa, coffee, cashew, tea, and rubber
  • Plantation crops are classified as beverage, oil-yielding, nut, industrial, or masticatory crops
  • Ornamental plants are grown for decoration; their cultivation is called floriculture
  • Herbaceous ornamentals have soft stems (annuals, biennials, perennials, aquatics); woody ornamentals have hard stems
  • Bedding plants are categorised as hardy annuals, tender annuals, hardy biennials, and corms or tubers
  • Key orchid genera in PNG include Vanda, Dendrobium, and Vanilla — all ornamentally important
