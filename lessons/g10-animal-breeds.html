Breeds of Monogastric & Polygastric Animals

📖 3 lessons 🇵🇬 Papua New Guinea Curriculum
Characteristics of Monogastric & Polygastric Animals Physiology: Digestive & Reproductive Systems Online Marketing of Animal Products

Learning Objectives

  • Compare and discuss the characteristics which distinguish monogastric and polygastric animals
  • Analyse and explain the physiology systems and functions of monogastric and polygastric animals
  • Identify types of technology that enable online marketing of animal products

Characteristics of Monogastric and Polygastric Animals

The characteristics of monogastric and polygastric animals depend mainly on differences in their size and their digestive system.

Monogastric Animals

Monogastric animals have a single stomach compartment and many are omnivorous in their feeding behaviour — meaning they eat both plant matter and animal matter. Examples:

  • Pigs — eat both plant matter and animal matter
  • Rabbits — eat mainly plant matter

Key characteristics of monogastric animals: single-chambered stomach; omnivores in eating habits.

Polygastric Animals

Polygastric animals have two or more storage compartment stomachs and are herbivorous in their feeding behaviour — meaning they consume mainly plant matter. Examples:

  • Cattle — farmed for meat and milk
  • Goats — farmed for meat and milk
  • Sheep — farmed for meat

Key characteristics of polygastric animals: larger body size; four-chambered stomach; rumen is the largest chamber; more efficient digestive system; consume mainly plant matter.

Key Differences

  • Polygastrics are bigger in size compared to monogastric animals
  • Polygastrics have a more efficient digestive system
  • Polygastrics mainly consume plant matter; monogastrics are omnivorous

Physiology: Skeletal, Digestive, and Reproductive Systems

Animal physiology is the scientific study of the life-supporting properties, functions, and processes of animals. The systems most important to a farmer are the skeletal, digestive, and reproductive systems.

Skeletal System

The skeletal system is made of bones. Its functions are: providing a framework for the body where muscles are attached; protecting vital organs; and allowing movement. Skeletal systems do not differ greatly between monogastric and polygastric animals, though the functions of individual bones may vary (e.g. wings in birds are used for flying).

Digestive System

The digestive systems of polygastric animals are quite different from monogastric animals. Sheep and cattle have four stomachs — the first is called the rumen. Animals with four stomachs are called ruminants or polygastric animals. Animals like pigs and poultry have a single stomach and cannot digest cellulose because they do not have a rumen.

Reproductive System

Reproduction is necessary for the survival of any species. Understanding animal reproductive physiology enables the development of reproductive technologies like artificial insemination, embryo transfer, and sexing semen. By studying developmental biology, farmers can better understand crucial developmental periods during gestation and make appropriate adjustments in feeding and management.

Technology for Online Marketing of Animal Products

The livestock industry has become the most dynamic market globally, driven largely by demand growth and modern technology. More and more farmers are beginning to use technology and computers for marketing their products.

Computer programs have been designed for convenience to farmers — breeders can evaluate data, generate annual production reports, and market their animal products through websites and social media. Key technologies for online marketing include:

  • Computers and internet — for accessing online marketplaces and communicating with buyers
  • Social media platforms — Facebook and similar platforms widely used in PNG to advertise live animals and animal products
  • Farm management software — programs that allow farmers to track production data and link production records to online sales
  • Mobile phones — allow farmers in remote areas to receive payment digitally and manage sales transactions

The relationship between technology innovation, digital marketing, and livestock industry performance improves overall competitiveness both locally and internationally.

Key Terms

Monogastric animalAn animal with a single-chambered stomach that is omnivorous in feeding behaviour. Examples: pigs, rabbits.
Polygastric animalAn animal with a four-chambered stomach (ruminant) that is herbivorous in feeding behaviour. Examples: cattle, goats, sheep.
RumenThe largest stomach chamber in polygastric (ruminant) animals, where microbial fermentation of plant material takes place.
Skeletal systemThe system of bones that provides a framework for the body, protects vital organs, and allows movement.
Reproductive systemThe biological system responsible for the survival of the species through production of offspring.
Digital marketingThe use of internet, computers, and social media to market and sell animal products to buyers online.

📝 Summary — What You Should Know

  • Monogastric animals have one stomach and are omnivorous (e.g. pigs, rabbits)
  • Polygastric animals have four stomach chambers, are herbivorous, and are larger in size (e.g. cattle, goats, sheep)
  • The rumen is the largest stomach chamber in polygastric ruminant animals
  • The skeletal system provides a framework, protects organs, and allows movement
  • Monogastrics cannot digest cellulose because they lack a rumen
  • Online marketing of animal products uses social media, computers, farm software, and mobile payments
