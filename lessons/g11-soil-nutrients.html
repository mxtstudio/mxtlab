🌱 Strand 1: Crops · Unit 1

Soil Nutrients: Macro & Micro

📚 Grade 11 🌿 Agriculture

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the role of macro nutrients in plant growth and identify their deficiency signs
  • Distinguish between macro nutrients and micro nutrients
  • Describe the importance of soil nutrients for crop production

What Are Soil Nutrients?

Soil nutrients are substances that plants absorb from the soil to grow and reproduce. They are divided into macro nutrients (needed in large amounts) and micro nutrients (needed in small amounts). Both must be present for fertile soil.

Macro Nutrients

Non-Mineral Elements

Carbon (C), Hydrogen (H), and Oxygen (O) — always available from air and water; no deficiency signs.

Primary Nutrients

  • Nitrogen (N) — older leaves turn yellow or light green; slow, dwarfed growth.
  • Phosphorus (P) — older leaves develop purple colour; stunted growth.
  • Potassium (K) — older leaf margins turn yellow; stunted growth.

Secondary Nutrients

  • Magnesium (Mg) — yellowing between leaf veins on older leaves.
  • Calcium (Ca) — poor root and shoot growth in young plants.
  • Sulphur (S) — yellowing in both young and old leaves.

Micro Nutrients

  • Boron (B) — terminal buds die.
  • Copper (Cu) — dark green leaves and stunted growth.
  • Iron (Fe) — yellowing on the veins of leaves.
  • Manganese (Mn) — palm fronds stunted and deformed; slow growth.
  • Zinc (Zn) — yellowing between the veins of leaves.

Key Terms

Soil nutrientsSubstances that plants absorb from the soil in order to grow and reproduce.
Macro nutrientsNutrients required by plants in relatively large amounts — including non-mineral elements (C, H, O) and primary nutrients (N, P, K).
Micro nutrientsNutrients required by plants only in very small quantities, such as boron, copper, iron, manganese, and zinc.
Primary nutrientsThe three most critical macro nutrients: Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K).
Secondary nutrientsMacro nutrients required in smaller amounts: Magnesium (Mg), Calcium (Ca), and Sulphur (S).
Deficiency signsVisual symptoms on plants when a nutrient is lacking — such as yellowing of leaves or stunted growth.

Summary

  • Soil nutrients are divided into macro nutrients (large amounts) and micro nutrients (small amounts)
  • Primary macro nutrients: Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K)
  • Secondary macro nutrients: Magnesium (Mg), Calcium (Ca), Sulphur (S)
  • Micro nutrients include Boron, Copper, Iron, Manganese, and Zinc
  • Nitrogen deficiency causes older leaves to yellow or turn light green
  • Each nutrient deficiency produces specific visible symptoms on plants
